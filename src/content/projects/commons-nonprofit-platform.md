The Commons is a concept for a modular, open-source software platform designed for nonprofit organizations. Nonprofits in the United States spend $7-8 billion annually on software, and the space is dominated by for-profit companies operating at near 40% margins on money that was donated, granted, or appropriated with the expectation it would serve the mission. On top of that, the systems are often fragmented and incompatible, forcing organizations to cobble together tools that don't communicate with each other.

Rather than building on an off-the-shelf wiki or CMS platform, I decided to roll a lightweight custom site. A custom build means complete ownership of the UI and no fighting framework conventions to present the content the way it needs to be presented.

## The Vision

Most nonprofits piece together separate tools for case management, volunteer coordination, fundraising, grants management, and reporting. Data lives in silos. Staff spend hours on manual reconciliation. Board members can't see the full picture.

The Commons would provide a single, modular platform where organizations enable only the functionality they need. One constituent record visible across all functions. Donor data connected to program outcomes. Funder reports generated automatically. Board-level visibility without manual assembly.

The platform would be free, self-hosted, and governed by a 501(c)(3) nonprofit foundation with an independent board and no shareholders.

## Current State

The project is in the vision and documentation phase. The documentation site covers four areas:

- **The Problem** — Market fragmentation and its impact on nonprofit operations
- **Why Now** — Market timing and technology enabling factors
- **What We'd Build** — Product vision, module architecture, and roadmap
- **How We'd Do It** — Organizational structure, governance, and go-to-market strategy
